Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India | Former President

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Fondly known as the "Missile Man of India," he played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. He was a source of inspiration to millions, especially the youth, due to his humility, dedication, and love for knowledge.

Key Contributions:

  • Led the development of India's Agni and Prithvi missiles
  • Chief Scientist at DRDO and ISRO
  • Key figure in India's 1998 nuclear tests at Pokhran
  • Served as the 11th President of India (2002-2007)
  • Author of inspirational books like Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds

Dr. Kalam dedicated his life to education and national development. Even after serving as President, he continued to teach and inspire students until his final moments. He passed away on July 27, 2015, doing what he loved—delivering a lecture.
